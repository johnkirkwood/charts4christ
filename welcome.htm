Welcomes from the Bible:

The welcome when Jesus was born.

Luke 2:10 And the angel said unto them (the shepherds), Fear not: for, behold, I bring you good tidings of great joy, which shall be to all people.
11 For unto you is born this day in the city of David a Saviour, which is Christ the Lord.
13 And suddenly there was with the angel a multitude of the heavenly host praising God, and saying,
14 Glory to God in the highest, and on earth peace, good will toward men.

A welcome in heaven when Jesus was resurrected

Luke 8:40 And as Jesus returned, the multitude welcomed him; for
they were all waiting for him. (This quote is not refering to the time Jesus was welcomed in Heaven, but we can reason from the hearty welcome that His early followers, who understood the message and mission of Jesus only faintly, gave Him, to the tremendous celebration and exchamge of love that must have been given by His father and all the Heavenly hosts to the successful savior of the universe and all that is in it.)

A welcome on earth when Israel is restored.

Jeremiah 31:10 Hear the word of the LORD, O ye nations, and declare it in the isles afar off, and say, He that scattered Israel will gather him, and keep him, as a shepherd doth his flock.
11 For the LORD hath redeemed Jacob, and ransomed Jacob from the hand of him that was stronger than Jacob.
12 Therefore Israel shall come and sing in the height of Zion, and shall flow together to the goodness of the LORD, for wheat, and for wine, and for oil, and for the young of the flock and of the herd: and their soul shall be as a watered garden; and they shall not sorrow any more at all.
