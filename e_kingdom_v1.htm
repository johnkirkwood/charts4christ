Feature Subject: Restitution

Several aspects of restitution will be examined in this issue of His Kingdom Comes. What restitution is, why we look forward to the "times of restitution",what we can expect before and during these times, all based on the Bible and Bible study. First we will examine what is being restored and thereby discover what restitution is.

Restitution; what is it?

Restitution is the return or the restoring of something lost or removed. If you restore an automobile or a cabinet, you bring it back to the condition that it had when it was new or the condition that the designer intended. Here in Exodus 22:6 restitution (payment) is required for the damage caused by a fire gone out of control. "If fire break out, and catch in thorns, so that the stacks of corn, or the standing corn, or the field, be consumed therewith; he that kindled the fire shall surely make restitution." In Acts 3:21 we find the key to the sort of restitution we will consider here; "Whom the heaven must receive until the times of restitution of all things, which God hath spoken by the mouth of all his holy prophets since the world began.". These "times of restitution of all things" God spoke of through His prophet Isaiah. Here are some of the statements which Isaiah made about this time period:

Isaiah 65:21 "And they shall build houses, and inhabit them; and they shall plant vineyards, and eat the fruit of them.
22 They shall not build, and another inhabit; they shall not plant, and another eat: for as the days of a tree are the days of my people, and mine elect shall long enjoy the work of their hands.
23 They shall not labour in vain, nor bring forth for trouble; for they are the seed of the blessed of the LORD, and their offspring with them.
24 And it shall come to pass, that before they call, I will answer; and while they are yet speaking, I will hear. 25 The wolf and the lamb shall feed together, and the lion shall eat straw like the bullock: and dust shall be the serpent's meat. They shall not hurt nor destroy in all my holy mountain, saith the LORD."
 
Isaiah 35:1 "The wilderness and the solitary place shall be glad for them; and the desert shall rejoice, and blossom as the rose.
2 It shall blossom abundantly, and rejoice even with joy and singing: the glory of Lebanon shall be given unto it, the excellency of Carmel and Sharon, they shall see the glory of the LORD, and the excellency of our God. 3 Strengthen ye the weak hands, and confirm the feeble knees.
4 Say to them that are of a fearful heart, Be strong, fear not: behold, your God will come with vengeance, even God with a recompence; he will come and save you.
5 Then the eyes of the blind shall be opened, and the ears of the deaf shall be unstopped.
6 Then shall the lame man leap as an hart, and the tongue of the dumb sing: for in the wilderness shall waters break out, and streams in the desert.
7 And the parched ground shall become a pool, and the thirsty land springs of water: in the habitation of dragons, where each lay, shall be grass with reeds and rushes.
8 And an highway shall be there, and a way, and it shall be called The way of holiness; the unclean shall not pass over it; but it shall be for those: the wayfaring men, though fools, shall not err therein.
9 No lion shall be there, nor any ravenous beast shall go up thereon, it shall not be found there; but the redeemed shall walk there:
10 And the ransomed of the LORD shall return, and come to Zion with songs and everlasting joy upon their heads: they shall obtain joy and gladness, and sorrow and sighing shall flee away."

A careful reading of these texts will reveal a new world that is wonderfully better and strikingly changed from the world that we know now. The statement; "The wolf and the lamb shall feed together," and "And they shall build houses, and inhabit them; and they shall plant vineyards, and eat the fruit of them." certainly speak of an Earthly kingdom not a Heavenly one since those animals, houses and vineyards are not invited into Heaven. But what does this have to do with restitution?

Romans 5:18 "Therefore as by the offence of one judgment came upon all men to condemnation; even so by the righteousness of one the free gift came upon all men unto justification of life.
19 For as by one man's disobedience many were made sinners, so by the obedience of one shall many be made righteous."

Only one man has ever lived a completely righteous life, Jesus. Adam had a chance to become righteous and failed in his attempt. However, while Adam was making his attempt, his environment, food, water, companions, yes, even all that he came into contact with, was the best that God had to offer. Likewise, when God extends His offer to "all men" to become righteous, God will present to "all men", the environment that He formerly gave to Adam. This giving again, or restoring, of an environment conducive to righteousness, is the "restitution" that we are discussing. Our degraded environment has been killing us since Adam and Eve were expelled from the Garden of Eden. The Garden of Eden environmental conditions are that which will be restored.

The lack of good sustenance (food, water and air), the lack of good education, the lack of good companionship all keep us from righteousness. All of these will be supplied when God restores the Garden of Eden conditions to the whole Earth. Here are some thoughts on what was lost and that which will be restored.

• Genesis 2:25 "And they were both naked, the man and his wife, and were not ashamed.
Genesis 2:5 ... for the LORD God had not caused it to rain upon the earth ...
6 But there went up a mist from the earth, and watered the whole face of the ground."
To me, this indicates that the weather in the garden of Eden was always warm, but not too warm, without rain or snow that would make Adam and Eve uncomfortable. Our future houses (Isaiah 65:21 "And they shall build houses, and inhabit them ...") will reflect these conditions by having no heating or cooling system. Therefore, with the absence of these power hungry systems, power generation could be done at the home site or at the community level making obsolete the megabusiness of central electric power generation, transportation and sales.
  • Genesis 2:16 "And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat:" This indicates that any and all of the fruit that grew in the garden would sustain life, perfect life. What a joy it will be to pick your own delicious food from your own varied trees, 12 months of the year, and eat it on the spot or in the house. It also indicates that the megabusiness of food growing (on commercial farms), food packaging, food transporting and food selling will be obsolete and at an END when the restitution takes place.
  • Genesis 2:15 "And the LORD God took the man, and put him into the garden of Eden to dress it and to keep it." This indicates that man's intended employment was that of dressing and keeping the garden (and any other necessary systems). What a difference from the repetitive, dreary, dead-end jobs that most of us have now.
  • Genesis 2:18 "And the LORD God said, It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him an help meet for him." I think every human that has ever lived feels in their heart the need for companionship. Not just the presence of someone else, but the closeness of others who are "soul-mates"; who aspire to the same beautiful goals; who give love to all; who are loved by all; whose touch is at once electric and soothing. Even our good friends the animals hunger for some communication and the touch of us and each other. I think that when both we and they are perfected, we will learn the languages of the animals and tell them, in the simplest of forms, what wonderful provisions God, through Jesus, has laid up for the Earth. We will radiate a love and peaceableness that the animals can sense.
  • Jeremiah 31:33 "But this shall be the covenant that I will make with the house of Israel; After those days, saith the LORD, I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts; and will be their God, and they shall be my people." Adam and Eve, when they were perfect, and Jesus, had a relationship with God, their soul-mate in the sky, that had all the elements of a good human relationship, save that of touch perhaps. The restitution of that relationship to all people of Earth, may be the most wonderful blessing of all. This promise was made to "the house of Israel" and it might seem that we, who are not Jews, are left out. We only have to look at the picture of the Fig tree to see that other branches (Christians) were grafted onto the Jewish Fig tree stem.
  • Genesis 28:12 "And he dreamed, and behold a ladder set up on the earth, and the top of it reached to heaven: and behold the angels of God ascending and descending on it." Here, in Jacob's dream, we see a symbolic picture of the future relationship of those on the Earth and those in Heaven. A direct line of communication with those in charge to erase completely any doubt about what the policies and procedures will be.

This wonderful world is not yet and has never been since Eden. Habakkuk 2:3 "For the vision is yet for an appointed time, but at the end it shall speak, and not lie: though it tarry, wait for it; because it will surely come, it will not tarry." When will that "appointed time" be?

To determine when we should look for restitution to take place, we need to consider where we are in the path of time to begin to see and understand the milestones and the signposts along the way. The following are some of the milestones and signposts that have stood the test of time and have been tried by those wishing to prove, to try by fire, the roadmap of our journey through time.

As watchers for His kingdom, we have the privilege, even the responsibility, to keep a vigilant eye on the signs of the times and the texts of the Bible to know where we are in Yahweh's time and in His plan.

 The Seventy Years of Desolation, 70 years

   This brings us to the period of the desolation of the land, which lasted 70 years, and was ended by the restoration of its people from Babylon, in the first year of Cyrus, B.C. 536 (See 2 Chron. 36:20,23), a date well established in secular history, and beyond which the line of Bible chronology does not extend.

From the Restoration to A.D. 1873, 536 years +1872 years

   The period from the time of the restoration of the Jews from Babylon, at the close of the 70 years desolation of their land, in the first year of Cyrus, down to the date known as A.D. 1, is not covered by Bible history. But, as before stated, it is well established by secular history as a period of 536 years. Ptolemy, a learned Greek-Egyptian, a geometer and astronomer, has well established these figures. They are generally accepted by scholars, and known as Ptolemy's Canon.

• Jesus gave all humanity the opportunity to KNOW that which is just, right, kind, true and loving.

• We shall soon see "the times of restitution of all things" when God will establish, through Christ and his Bride, the New Covenant Government.
 
• Jesus Christ and the consecrated 144,000, Christ's bride, the Saints, those who, in loving Jesus, suffered and died for Him, form the core of the New Covenant Government.
 
• At the end of the 1000 year (Millennial) Day God will then choose those worthy of eternal life.
